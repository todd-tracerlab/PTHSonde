1

Choose a frequency

Pick a channel in the 902–928 MHz band. Both the sonde and receiver must end up on the same one. Band-center (915 MHz) is a good default.

2

Tune the receiver

Sets the channel this ground station listens on. Telemetry only appears when the receiver is on the same channel as the sonde.

3

Send the channel to the sonde (pre-launch)

Over-the-air command — the sonde must be powered on and nearby. It keeps transmitting on the control channel but stages the new one, then reports it back so you can confirm before committing.

4

Lock & launch

Final step. The sonde switches to the staged channel and stops listening for commands for the rest of the flight (clears only on power-cycle). The receiver follows automatically.

?

Lost the sonde / don't know its channel?

Sweeps the whole band listening for any valid packet and locks onto whatever channel it finds. Use this on recovery day or if a launch went sideways.
